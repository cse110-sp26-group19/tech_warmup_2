## Core Design Principles

- House maintains a mathematical edge to be profitable

- For every $100 the player wagers, they earn back ~$96 — a 4% margin that keeps the player coming back

- Referrals and prizes to get more people hooked onto the slot game

- Spin button must be the most important designed element — should be animated and addictive to keep players returning

- Win animation should trigger a dopamine response (lots of color, coins, energy)

- Big jackpot numbers clearly visible at all times to keep players hooked on the idea of winning big

- Needs a cohesive theme and aesthetic — Ancient cultures, space, fruits, retro, fantasy, dragons (mascot)

## Addictive Nature — Sources

**Variable Ratio Reinforcement**
- [Lumen Learning – Introduction to Psychology](https://courses.lumenlearning.com/waymaker-psychology/chapter/reading-reinforcement-schedules/)
- Shows how rewards delivered on an unpredictable schedule create strong behavioral conditioning
- Triggers dopamine responses even for near-wins, keeping users hooked

**Money Abstraction**
- [The Ludicrous Psychology of Slot Machines – Trung Phan](https://www.readtrung.com/p/the-ludicrous-psychology-of-slot)
- Trading real money for "tokens" makes players feel as though they aren't spending real money
- Reduces psychological friction → players buy more credits
